What is IP Tracking?

IP tracking is like a digital postmark. When you visit a website, your device automatically sends its "address" (called an IP address) to that website. The website can then use this address to figure out roughly where you are located.

Simple explanation:
  • An IP address is like a digital address label
  • Each time you visit a website, your device shares that label with the server
  • The site can use it to estimate your city or region
  • But it does not reveal your exact street address or your real name

How Does It Work?

Here's the simple process:

  1. You visit a website: When you type a website address, your browser sends a request.
  2. Your IP is included: This request includes your IP address automatically.
  3. The website receives it: The website's server gets your request and records your IP.
  4. Location is found: The website looks up your IP in a database to find your approximate location (usually city-level).

Important note
