Image tracking represents one of the most versatile and powerful methods for gathering user analytics. Unlike traditional URL-based tracking, image tracking works seamlessly across platforms, devices, and contexts where users may not directly click links.

How Image Tracking Works

When a tracking image is loaded on any platform - whether in emails, websites, social media, or messaging apps - it automatically sends valuable data back to the tracking server:

Geographic Data

Precise location information including country, region, city, and coordinates

Device Detection

Complete device fingerprinting including OS, browser, screen resolution

Security & Stealth Techniques

Effective image tracking requires balancing visibility with functionality:

  • Invisible Sizing: 1x1 pixel dimensions ensure tracking remains undetectable
  • Transparent Images: Using transparent PNG files maintains invisibility
  • CSS Hiding: Additional styling ensures tracking images don't affect layout
  • Alt Text Removal: Preventing accessibility tools from announcing tracking elements
